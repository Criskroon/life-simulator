# Deep Audit — 2026-04-25

Run on **1000 lives per pass × 2 passes = 2,000 lives**, master seed 7. Generated by `testing/agents/deepAudit.ts` using the activities-aware simulator at `testing/lib/simulatorWithActivities.ts`. Total runtime: ~0.8s.

The two passes:
- **No-AI**: original behaviour — simulator only resolves events, never picks activities.
- **With-AI**: per year, 60% chance the simulated player engages the Activities menu. When engaged, the AI picks weighted-randomly from eligible+affordable activities, with light category biases (Mind&Body when stats are low, Career&Money when employed, Love&Social when friend-light), and refuses to do the same activity more than twice in one year.

## Section B — Critical Issues (need fixing)

### B1. ⚠ Activity-spawned relationships pile up duplicate-id entries
Confirmed by a 1,000-life targeted run (`/tmp/dupcheck.mjs`):

> **1,934 duplicate-id occurrences across 1,000 lives** — virtually every life has at least one duplicate, often several.
>
> Worst real example (life 0): `rel-gym-friend` appears **6 times** as "Sam Park / friend"; `rel-activity-partner` appears 3 times as "Alex Rivera / partner"; `rel-vacation-romance` appears twice as "Casey Moreno / partner".

Root cause: the `addRelationship` activity payloads in [src/game/data/activities.ts](src/game/data/activities.ts) use stable hard-coded IDs (`rel-gym-friend`, `rel-vacation-romance`, `rel-activity-partner`, `rel-library-friend`). The handler in [effectsApplier.ts:41-44](src/game/engine/effectsApplier.ts:41) appends without dedupe:
```ts
addRelationship: (state, payload) => {
  return { ...state, relationships: [...state.relationships, rel] };
},
```
Same activity firing twice → duplicate entries. Counter-friction in the AI caps it at 2/year, but across a 70-year life, gym fires many times.

**Player-visible consequence:** when `find_date` rolls outcome A then later outcome D, the player ends up with two distinct partners (`Alex Rivera` AND `Morgan Bell`) sharing id `rel-activity-partner`. `rel_propose` then sees "has partner" = true; if the player marries, only one partner is "consumed" by id and the other remains as a phantom partner alongside the new spouse.

### B2. ⚠ `career_burnout` unreachable when activities are enabled
Triggered in 4.0% of lives no-AI but **0.0% of lives with-AI**. Conditions are `job != null` AND `stats.happiness < 35`. The Activities-AI keeps mean final happiness at 97.7 vs 64.6 in the no-AI pass; nobody ever drops to <35. So the entire burnout content branch (and its sabbatical / push-through choice tree) is invisible to engaged players.

### B3. ⚠ `career_fired` triggered in only 0.6% / 0.0% of lives
Conditions are `job.performance < 30`. New jobs initialise performance at 60–65 (verified at [career.ts:25](src/game/data/events/career.ts:25), [career.ts:61](src/game/data/events/career.ts:61), [career.ts:541](src/game/data/events/career.ts:541)). The only event that meaningfully tanks performance is `career_bad_review` (–8 max per fire), and `career_workplace_romance` / `career_long_hours` push it the other way. Across a career, performance almost never crosses below 30. Same content-buried-by-balance issue as B2.

### B4. ⚠ Single-year graduation events have low conversion
Each major graduation event only fires within a 1-year age window:

| Event | Window | Conversion (started → graduated, with-AI pass) |
|---|---|---|
| `edu_graduate_high_school` | age 18 | 192 / 473 enrolled = **40.6%** |
| `edu_uni_graduate` | age 22 | 30 / 104 enrolled = **28.8%** |
| `edu_grad_school_complete` | age 26-30 (5y) | 4 / 10 enrolled = **40%** |

Conversion is gated by event-selector roll: 30% of years pick zero events, and category-diversity downweights education to 0.25× when an education event already fired earlier the same tick. With weight 20.0 the graduation is the dominant edu pick when picked at all, but a 1-year window plus quiet-year + diversity penalty leaves substantial dropouts who *should* have graduated. The grad-school chain has 5 years to compensate; high-school and university don't.

### B5. ⚠ `edu_uni_party_phase` virtually unreachable (0.9% in both passes)
Conditions: `education has university`, age 19-22. With only ~3-10% of lives enrolled in university *and* the event competing in the same 4-year window with `edu_uni_graduate` (weight 20) and various career/relationship events, this 0.7-weight event is starved out.

## Section E — Design Observations (FYI, no action needed)

These were observed in the data but may be intentional. Listed for awareness only.

- **The mortality curve is health-coupled.** `endYear()` rolls `chance × (1 + (100 − health) / 50)`, so dropping health from 100 to 0 triples the mortality probability per year. With activities pinning health near 100, the with-AI pass extends mean lifespan modestly (3 years), not dramatically.

- **Action budget peaks at SENIOR** (age ≥ 65, base 4 actions/year, +1 if unemployed → clamped to MAX 5). Retirees genuinely have the most activity slots. This is documented behaviour in [actionBudget.ts](src/game/engine/actionBudget.ts) but it does mean post-retirement is the most "activity-spammable" phase. Edge-case verification (10 cases) confirms the formula matches the spec, including the `MIN_BUDGET=2` floor that makes the "low health" penalty invisible for already-floored states.

- **Senior 85, sick** still gets budget 3 (4 base − 1 sick − 1 old = 2, but the +1 unemployed bonus pushes back to 3). Not a bug — but a player may notice that "very sick at 85 still has 3 free actions per year".

- **`oncePerLife: true` is rare in the catalog.** Most events can re-fire repeatedly; that's why "weird dream" hits 94%+ — not "1 in 100 lives" but "every life over 70+ years". This is a deliberate design choice (BitLife does the same), but it strongly amplifies the dominance of high-weight, no-condition `random.ts` events.

- **`rel_blind_date` *drops* from 57.1% to 25.7%** in the with-AI pass. That's because `find_date` (an activity) seeds a `partner` early; `rel_blind_date` requires `relationships lacks 'partner'`. The trigger frequency drop is entirely expected — players who *engage* with dating naturally won't see "your friend wants to set you up on a blind date" as often. Working as designed.

- **`rel_friendship_drifts` jumps from 16.9% to 77.8%** with-AI. Same dynamic in reverse: activities (gym, library, vacation) seed friendships, which in turn unlock the friendship-related events. Activities aren't just stat changes — they unlock content branches.

- **Country gating is one hardcoded `if`** in `activityEngine.ts:31` for `lottery_ticket`. Adding a second gated activity today would silently bypass the gate (the function returns `true` for unknown ids).

## Section F — Recommendations

### Top 3 to tackle first (Cristoffer)

1. **Fix B1: addRelationship duplicate IDs.** Two viable paths:
   - Cheapest: change the `addRelationship` handler at [effectsApplier.ts:41](src/game/engine/effectsApplier.ts:41) to skip-if-id-exists. Existing data shape is preserved; activities just become idempotent.
   - More correct: have activities mint unique IDs at apply-time (e.g. `rel-gym-friend-${state.currentYear}`) so a player can collect multiple gym friends over a life. Requires authoring a new effect shape.
   - Suggest path #1 unless multiple friends-from-the-same-source is desired content.

2. **Address B2 + B3: unreachable career content.** Either lower the difficulty bar (`career_burnout` at happiness < 50 instead of < 35; `career_fired` at performance < 50 instead of < 30) or add an event that explicitly tanks happiness/performance at modest probability. Current state means two careers' worth of authored content is dead unless the player plays badly on purpose.

3. **Fix B4: graduation conversion**, especially for high-school (40% → ~60-70% target). Cheapest fix: extend the age window from 1 year to 2-3 years (e.g. age 17-19 for HS graduation). The `oncePerLife: true` flag already prevents double-graduations.

### Top 3 to defer

1. **Activity stat-cap saturation.** The with-AI pass pins health/happiness/smarts at ~98. Tuning per-activity yields downward (e.g. gym +4 → +2 health) is a balance pass, not a blocker — the engine works correctly, it's just that the player has too many free wins. Defer until you have more events that *use* the high stats.
2. **Promotion event not updating `job.title`.** Cosmetic only; level and salary update correctly. Worth bundling with any future career-events refactor.
3. **`passesCountryGate` is hardcoded for one activity id.** The file comments already plan the generalization (`requireRule?: keyof CountryRules`). Land it when the second gated activity ships.
